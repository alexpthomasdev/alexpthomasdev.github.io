This person is sensitive and possesses a desire to get things done quickly and accurately. They have creative ability, combined with an attention to detail. A seemingly inexhaustible investigator of facts, this person will pursue all the possible avenues when solving a problem. This process results in a flow of different and often creative ideas.

A perfectionist, their drive for tangible accomplishments is counter-balanced by an equal drive to maintain correctness. This person needs someone to tap their creative flow. They need assistance in bringing projects to completion.

This person tends to resent restrictions. They need an understanding supervisor who appreciates that they do not stop thinking about an assignment, even after work. When it comes to decision making they may vacillate and procrastinate.

Conscientiousness

This person is conscientious and values high standards in their work and the work of others. They will enjoy seeing a job well done because of the satisfaction it brings. This person will take put a lot of effort into all aspects of their work to ensure that it is of a high quality. Compared to others, they will find it easy to motivate themselves and they will rarely procrastinate, even if something does not interest them. A sense of achievement will be engaging for them and they will often set themselves goals and targets. This person is more likely than others to strive for success and they will probably put in extra time to improve the quality of their work, even when it is not required.

Adjustment

This person is exceptionally composed, even under extreme pressure. They will rarely show signs of stress or the experience of negative emotions. This person will have these feelings at times, but they will find it easy to remain focused and will not entertain negative emotions for long. Compared to most people, they will be confident in their work and self-assured in their ability to overcome setbacks. This person’s extremely composed nature can make hard for others to know how they are really feeling. If this person rarely shows their emotions, others may view them as cold, aloof, and unapproachable. On the other hand, their calm and collected demeanour will give people confidence during challenging times and they may be reliably composed in a crisis.

Curiosity

This person has extremely high levels of curiosity, actively seeking out new ideas, experiences, and opportunities wherever they can. They may find that their interests change quickly as they try out new things or interact with new people. It is highly likely that this person will enjoy learning and questioning everything. They will want to know what other people think about a range of topics and why they agree or disagree with their own views. When things around them change, this person will be more likely than most to embrace the novelty and adapt quickly. Due to their curiosity, them may get bored very quickly with repetitive or mundane tasks. When they work with others, they will be engaged by lively debate and will be happy going off on a tangent. Theis person will enjoy hearing lots of views, even if they conflict with their own. They may not agree with everyone, but they will value and appreciate different perspectives that enrich their own understanding of something.

Risk Approach

This person is exceptionally proactive when it comes to interpersonal issues. They will want to resolve any problems immediately, taking a candid and forthright approach with others. They will be very comfortable in a confrontation and will view tough conversations as an efficient way to get things done. This person may find that some people greatly appreciate their frank and direct style, however most people are not as comfortable with conflict as they are. When dealing with others, this person will value frank discussions and candour.

Ambiguity Acceptance

This person will be comfortable with ambiguity and complexity in the world around them. Typically, they will view uncertainty as interesting and challenging. It is likely that they can solve abstract problems and enjoy thinking about complex concepts that do not have simple explanations. When this person is in an ambiguous situation, they will adapt quickly, even if they only have limited information. That comfort with uncertainty can be advantageous when they must deal with change, or unexpected problems. This person will probably enjoy strategic thinking and scenario planning. At times, their comfort with ambiguity might lead them to act on partial information, rather than following structured plans. They may find rigid, authoritarian environments difficult and will probably benefit from some guidelines and processes to help them stay on track.

Competitiveness

This person is not very competitive, having an aversion to competitive situations. They may find competitive and target-driven environments to be frustrating and demotivating. This person will judge their success based on their own evaluations, rarely needing external praise and recognition. They will appreciate sincere recognition of their work and will often take the time to acknowledge where other people helped them succeed. They will be a committed team player who wants everyone to succeed together, greatly disliking win or lose, 'winner takes all' approaches. Compared to most people, they will not seek glory and they will often champion the achievements of others over their own.

Communication Style

behaviour

This person is likely to be a direct and specific communicator who communicates through facts, figures and logic. They may prefer written communication such as an email. They may occasionally encounter difficulty in winning over others and may fall back on their direct, authoritative or specialist approach. They may come across to others as serious by nature which is likely to be effective in important meetings; however, their serious nature could also be seen as lacking in enthusiasm.

Decision Making

behaviour

Whilst this person has characteristics which suggest that they can be very decisive within their area of knowledge, they do have an innate desire to get things right. Therefore these two aspects may well be at variance with each other and this could cause this person to appear indecisive and to vacillate at critical times.

How To Motivate

behaviour

Whilst this person is motivated by power, authority and challenge, it is also important that there are no sudden or abrupt changes in the work direction and that operating procedures are very clearly defined. Despite this person's assertive attitude, it may be necessary at times to give reassurance in order to keep them well motivated.

Should this person have a boss, then ideally that person will be a direct and democratic individual who deals in facts, figures and logic and has a systematic approach in motivating subordinates. The boss should remember that there may be times when as a result of this person's high dominance and compliance, it may be necessary to assist them in the decision making process, especially if decisions occur outside this person's area of expertise.

How To Manage

behaviour

The key to managing this person is time and explanation. Their boss must understand that they will want to seek the perfect solution to a problem or produce the best product or result that is possible. To do this, this person needs time to investigate and research to test and assess. Thus, where possible, tight time schedules should be avoided. In addition, they need to know exactly what is wanted. Vague guidelines or broad brush pictures makes this person feel anxious, irritated and annoyed. The result will often be one of logical, defensive antagonism. Given thorough explanation and reasonable time, they will more often than not react far more quickly than expected.

The manager should also be able to differentiate between guidelines and restrictions. Once this person knows what is required, what resources are available and the limits to their responsibility, they prefer to be left to get on with the job. One of the reasons they often complete tasks more quickly than one would expect is because they don't stop thinking about or working on a problem when the normal working day ends.

How To Onboard

behaviour

The best way of supporting this inquisitive and practically creative individual is to provide direction for their creative flow. Show them where their ideas can be used. Additionally provide them with the time and resources to investigate and re-examine both their proposed solutions and ideas.

Another area where this individualist may require support is in their relationships with and understanding of, colleagues whose interests and expertise differ from their own. The manager can assist by de-formalising lines of communication and explaining how the differing individual inputs contribute to an effective whole.

Maximising Potential

  • Be sure the work parameters and procedures are clearly defined
  • Be prepared to provide help and reassurance, as this person may vacillate when decisions need to be made
  • Set realistic timescales as they may miss deadlines because of their need for the 'perfect' result
  • Realise that they prefer communication to be in writing rather than verbal
  • Give this person technical problems rather than people-problems
  • Be aware that they can confuse non-specialist people with too much detailed technical information

Leadership Style

behaviour

This person is likely to have leadership potential in strategic, technical, specialist or operational areas. They will tend to work best in an organisation whose culture is direct and structured. They are likely to prefer being set goals which need to be achieved in a logical, accurate way. They would be most likely to succeed in an organisational culture that is direct, demanding and logical. However, they may, at times, be too direct and blunt in their approach for some people.

However, their leadership potential will also depend on aspects of their emotional awareness and level of fluid intelligence.

Leadership Strengths

  • Competitive in their area of expertise
  • Orientated toward problem-solving, fact-finding, trouble-shooting and planning
  • Complies with policies and procedures
  • Objectively evaluates situations
  • Focused on quality standards and results
  • Takes objective rather than emotional decisions
